The standard approach to hosting capacity studies has a fundamental flaw: it treats every feeder as a static snapshot in time. But distribution networks aren't static — they change with load patterns, weather, DER adoption, and topology modifications.

## The Problem with Static Analysis

Most utilities run hosting capacity studies using a single snapshot of the feeder: peak load, maximum DER penetration, worst-case line configuration. This creates two problems:

1. **Over-engineering:** Recommendations are based on worst-case scenarios that rarely occur simultaneously, leading to unnecessary upgrade costs
2. **Under-confidence:** A static analysis can't tell you how hosting capacity changes as conditions evolve, making it less useful for long-term planning

## A Better Approach

We've found that combining time-series analysis with Monte Carlo simulation produces more actionable results. Instead of a single hosting capacity number, you get:

- Hosting capacity distribution (not just a point estimate)
- Sensitivity to key variables (load, weather, topology)
- Confidence intervals for interconnection decisions
- Dynamic thresholds that adapt as grid conditions change

## The Results

In a recent engagement with a regional utility, this approach:

- Reduced unnecessary upgrade recommendations by 40%
- Cut interconnection study turnaround from 8 weeks to 3 weeks
- Gave the utility a dynamic dashboard that updated as conditions changed

## What This Means for You

If you're still running hosting capacity studies on static snapshots, you're either overbuilding (wasting money) or under-informing your decision-makers (losing opportunities). A dynamic approach gives you both accuracy and agility.
